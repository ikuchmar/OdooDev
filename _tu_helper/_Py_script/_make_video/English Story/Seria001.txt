Seria001 — History of England

1.1 Celtic Tribes.

EN:
History of England: Celtic Tribes.
Celtic tribes lived in Britain before the Romans came.
They built villages and farmed the land.
They grew crops like wheat and barley.
They raised animals such as cows and sheep.
Many people lived in round houses with thatched roofs.
Celtic warriors used swords, shields, and spears.
They painted their bodies before battle.
Hillforts on high ground protected their communities.
Druids were priests, teachers, and judges.
They believed in many gods of nature.
Festivals marked the seasons of the year.
Celtic art showed beautiful patterns and symbols.
Their traditions influenced later British culture.

1.2 Roman Britain.

EN:
History of England: Roman Britain.
In 43 AD the Romans invaded Britain.
They defeated the Celtic tribes and took control.
The Romans built towns with stone houses and baths.
Hadrian’s Wall was built to keep out northern tribes.
They made strong roads that connected the country.
Latin words entered the local languages.
Roman soldiers lived in forts across the land.
Villas with mosaics showed Roman wealth.
People visited theatres and amphitheatres for entertainment.
Roman law brought order and discipline.
Trade increased with other parts of the empire.
Christianity began to spread in Britain.
The Romans stayed for almost 400 years.
Their influence is still seen today.

1.3 Anglo-Saxons.

EN:
History of England: Anglo-Saxons.
After the Romans left, the Anglo-Saxons arrived.
They came from Germany, Denmark, and the Netherlands.
They sailed in wooden boats across the sea.
The Anglo-Saxons lived in Britain from the 5th to the 11th century.
They built villages and farmed the land.
They divided the country into small kingdoms.
Famous kingdoms were Wessex, Mercia, and Northumbria.
Their houses were made of wood and straw.
They spoke Old English, the root of modern English.
Many common words today come from their language.
They worshipped many gods before Christianity spread.
Monks later built monasteries and schools.
The Anglo-Saxons wrote epic poems like Beowulf.
They left a strong mark on English culture.

It also influenced democracy in other countries.

It showed that even kings must follow rules.
Its spirit shaped the future of England.

Henry VIII had six wives and no male heir.
He created the Church of England.
Monasteries were closed and their land taken.
His daughter Mary tried to restore Catholic faith.
Elizabeth I ruled for many years.
Her reign is called the Golden Age.
England defeated the Spanish Armada in 1588.
Explorers sailed to the Americas and Asia.
Trade and culture expanded during this time.
Shakespeare wrote plays and poems.
The Tudors made England strong and powerful.
